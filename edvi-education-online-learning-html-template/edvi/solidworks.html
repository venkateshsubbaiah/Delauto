    Solidworks

Course Description:

This course provides an extensive exploration of SolidWorks, a leading CAD software for mechanical design and simulation. Participants will learn how to create complex 3D models, assemblies, and detailed drawings. The course also covers the simulation tools within SolidWorks, enabling students to analyze and optimize their designs for performance and manufacturability.

Course Objectives:

  • To impart comprehensive skills in using SolidWorks for mechanical design and 3D modeling.

  • To develop proficiency in creating and managing complex assemblies and detailed part designs.

  • To prepare students for professional applications of SolidWorks in various engineering fields.

    • Course Content:

    Introduction to SolidWorks • Overview of SolidWorks interface and key features • Setting up projects and file management • Basic sketching and part modeling • Customizing toolbars and shortcuts
    Fundamentals of Part Modeling • Detailed 3D part modeling techniques • Using features like extrude, revolve, loft, and sweep • Applying relations, constraints, and parametric design • Creating and managing part configurations
    Assembly Modeling • Building and managing complex assemblies • Using mates and assembly constraints • Detecting and resolving assembly conflicts • Exploring advanced assembly features (e.g., smart fasteners, toolbox)
    Detailed Drawing and Documentation • Generating detailed 2D drawings from 3D models • Adding dimensions, annotations, and tolerances • Creating exploded views and assembly instructions • Utilizing BOM (Bill of Materials) and design tables
    Final Project and Review • Capstone project: Complete a mechanical design with simulations and detailed documentation • Peer review and feedback sessions • Course summary and Q&A
    Assessment Methods: • Weekly assignments and hands-on exercises. • Mid-term practical exam. • Final project presentation and evaluation.

    Learning Outcomes:

    By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Navigate the SolidWorks interface and use its design and simulation tools effectively.

  • Create detailed 3D models and assemblies, applying best practices for mechanical design.

  • Generate professional-grade technical drawings with annotations, dimensions, and tolerances.

  • Conduct simulations to analyze mechanical performance, including stress, motion, and thermal analysis.

  • Optimize designs for manufacturability and cost-efficiency.

  • Integrate SolidWorks with other engineering tools and workflows.
